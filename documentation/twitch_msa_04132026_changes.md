# Twitch MSA (Monitized Streamer Agreement) April 13th, 2026 Update
Summarized high level overview of the updated Twitch MSA that was updated on April 13th, 2026 and goes into binding effect on May 13th, 2026
[Twitch Legal - Monitized Streamer Agreement](https://legal.twitch.com/legal/monetized-streamer-agreement/)

## Purpose and scope
- This Agreement governs your participation in Twitch’s Monetized Streamer Program, which gives you access to monetization tools on your Twitch channel.
- It applies to any person or entity that uses or attempts to use these tools, and it sits on top of the standard Twitch Terms of Service (which still apply to all use of Twitch).

## Eligibility and compliance (Section 1)
- You must submit a complete, accurate application and be approved by Twitch; eligibility is determined at Twitch’s sole discretion.
- If Twitch rejects your application or terminates your account for violations, you cannot rejoin the Program without Twitch’s written permission.
- You must keep all account details (birthday, email, address, channel info) accurate and up to date.
- You must comply with this Agreement and the Terms of Service to earn any Program Fees; Twitch can disable ads, withhold fees, terminate the Agreement, or disable your channel for non‑compliance or fraud (e.g., fake ad views/clicks).

## Content responsibilities (Section 2)
- You are the “executive producer” of your channel and fully responsible for all content you stream or program on Twitch.
- Twitch emphasizes that Twitch is a live‑streaming service: your content should primarily be live, and you should generally engage viewers through chat or other live means when you’re live.
- You must obtain and maintain all necessary rights and permissions for everything you broadcast (music, games, media, etc.) and promptly remove anything that violates the Terms of Service.

## Advertising‑related data sharing (Section 3)
- To provide monetization tools and run ads on your channel, Twitch may share certain information with Amazon, advertisers, and service providers.
- Shared data includes:
  - Public channel info (name, bio, visible channel details).
  - Stream metadata (categories, tags).
  - Aggregated audience reach info (primary language, hours streamed, viewer size by country).
- By agreeing to this Agreement, you explicitly consent to that data sharing while you are in the Program.
- If you want to withdraw this consent, you must terminate the Agreement and leave the Program; you can still stream on Twitch, but not as a monetized streamer under this Program.
- Even without the Agreement, some data sharing may still occur under Twitch’s general Privacy Notice, but this section specifically governs monetization‑driven sharing.

## Program revenue and tools (Section 4)
- Twitch retains the exclusive right to monetize the platform; you only earn where Twitch exposes specific “Program Tools.”
- If you are not a Twitch Affiliate or Partner, you are ineligible to receive Program Fee payouts, even though you may still use monetization tools and accrue fees on your dashboard.
- Your eligible tools and specific fee rates are displayed on your Creator Dashboard, which is incorporated into the Agreement by reference.

### Program Tools covered:
**Bits**
- You may earn a fee when viewers cheer with Bits in your channel.
- You must follow the Bits Acceptable Use Policy, including rules on how you solicit Bits.

**Channel Subscriptions**
- You earn a percentage of Net Revenue from paid and gifted subs; your exact percentage is shown on your dashboard.
- Twitch controls subscription products (tiers, bundles, pricing, local pricing, tokens).
- When viewers use Subscription Tokens (e.g., Prime), you receive a fixed‑rate fee per token based on the viewer’s country instead of a share of Net Revenue, and Twitch can change these fixed rates at any time.

**Advertisements**
- You may earn a percentage of Net Revenue from ads shown on or around your channel; your percentage is on your dashboard.
- Twitch is not obligated to run ads on your channel and retains control over ad format, type, and placement.

**Offers**
- From time to time, Twitch may provide opt‑in Offers (bounties, bonuses, special deals) with their own terms; if those conflict with this Agreement, the Offer terms control for that specific Offer.

## Payment, thresholds, and Spendable Balance (Section 5)

### Payment terms and Net Revenue
- Program Fees are paid monthly, within 45 days after the end of each calendar month, in USD unless your payment option says otherwise.
- “Net Revenue” is defined as revenue Twitch actually receives that is shareable with you minus platform and ad sales costs, licensing costs, taxes, refunds, chargebacks, discounts, currency conversion costs, bad debt, bank/transaction fees, and similar credits/expenses.
- You must provide all required tax and payment documentation (e.g., W‑9/W‑8); if you fail to do so within 180 days of fees becoming payable, those fees are forfeited on a rolling month‑by‑month basis until you comply.

### Thresholds and payout eligibility
- You only become entitled to a payout once you reach the Payment Threshold shown on your dashboard; Twitch has no obligation to pay out amounts below that threshold.
- The Payment Threshold itself is incorporated into the Agreement via the dashboard.
- To receive a payout outside Twitch, you must:
  - Be a Twitch Affiliate or Partner;
  - Have reached the Payment Threshold; and
  - Have opted into receiving payouts in your dashboard.

### Spendable Balance and account inactivity
- Program Fees shown on your dashboard (even below threshold) can be used as Spendable Balance to make certain purchases on Twitch (like Bits and gifted subs) if you opt in.
- Before you become Affiliate/Partner, your Program Fees are only accessible as Spendable Balance, not as cash withdrawals.
- If your account has no logged‑in activity for 2 years (or longer, if required by your local law), any unpaid Program Fees above the threshold may be treated as abandoned property or may expire under applicable law.

### Offsets, corrections, and estimates
- Twitch may offset any amounts you owe them against amounts they owe you (including Program Fees) across any Twitch agreements.
- If you are overpaid, Twitch can adjust or offset the overpayment against your Spendable Balance or future Program Fees.
- Amounts displayed on your dashboard are estimates for information only and may not match final payout amounts after full calculations and adjustments.

### Taxes
- You can charge Twitch certain applicable sales or VAT taxes if you are legally required to, provided they’re properly itemized on invoices.
- Twitch can withhold any taxes it is legally required to withhold from payments to you; the reduced payment is considered full settlement for that amount.
- You are responsible for all local tax compliance on income you receive under this Agreement.

## Representations and warranties (Section 6)
- You confirm that:
  - You are allowed to use Twitch and participate in the Program.
  - You’ve read and agree to the Terms of Service.
  - Your participation and receipt of fees won’t violate other contracts or laws.
  - All information you provide is and remains accurate.
- You also confirm you are not a “Sanctioned Person” (subject to U.S. sanctions lists or other similar restrictions).
- Twitch does not promise any specific amount of revenue or earnings.

## Term and termination (Section 7)
- The Agreement starts when you accept it and continues until terminated by you or Twitch.
- Either party can terminate for any reason with 30 days’ written notice.
- Twitch can also suspend or terminate immediately if:
  - You or your content violate the Terms of Service or this Agreement and your channel is suspended/banned;
  - You are a “repeat infringer” under Twitch’s DMCA policy and are banned;
  - You are or become a Sanctioned Person.
- Upon termination, most rights end, but specific sections (including payment, limitations, dispute resolution, etc.) survive; the general Terms of Service also continue.

## Modifications to the Agreement (Section 8)
- Twitch can change, add, or remove terms (“Modifications”) at its discretion, for reasons like product updates or law changes.
- Twitch will notify you via methods such as email, notices on Twitch, or dashboard messages.
- Changes apply prospectively and become effective 30 days after notification.
- If you don’t agree with changes, your only remedy is to terminate the Agreement; your continued participation 30+ days after notice counts as acceptance.

## Disclaimers, indemnity, and liability (Sections 9–11)
- Twitch provides the Program “as is,” with broad disclaimers of warranties (including implied warranties of merchantability, fitness, and non‑infringement) to the extent allowed by law.
- You agree to indemnify and hold Twitch harmless from claims, losses, and costs related to your actions, breaches, or use of the Program.
- Twitch and its affiliates are not liable for lost profits, indirect or consequential damages, and their total liability is capped at the revenue paid or payable to you under this Agreement in the 12 months before the event giving rise to the claim.

## Governing law and arbitration (Sections 12–13)
- The Agreement is governed by California law (without applying other jurisdictions’ conflicts‑of‑law rules).
- Most disputes must go to binding arbitration with JAMS, with limited discovery and no class‑wide damages or similar relief; some carve‑outs allow small claims cases and IP enforcement in court.
- If a dispute goes to court instead of arbitration, it must be in federal or state courts in San Francisco County, and you agree to that jurisdiction and venue.

## Miscellaneous (Section 14)
- If a loan‑out company signs on your behalf, you (the individual) agree to all terms, grant the rights, and back the company’s promises.
- Notices are delivered by email (to your program email or to Twitch’s legalnotice@twitch.tv).
- You and Twitch are independent contractors; the Agreement does not create an employment or agency relationship, and you cannot bind Twitch.
- You cannot assign the Agreement without Twitch’s written consent; Twitch can assign it to affiliates or in connection with corporate transactions.
- If part of the Agreement is invalid, the rest remains enforceable.
- This is the complete Agreement on this subject and supersedes prior monetization agreements, except where a specific Offer says otherwise.

## Big‑picture takeaway
- The Agreement formalizes “Monetization for All”: many creators can access monetization tools and accrue Program Fees from day one, but only Affiliates and Partners can actually cash out those earnings.
- It explicitly trades access to monetization tools for consent to share channel and audience data with Amazon and advertisers.
- It centralizes your actual percentages, thresholds, and payment behavior on the Creator Dashboard and binds those settings as part of the contract.
